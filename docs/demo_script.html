Live demo script
The 12-minute story
Each beat is timed. Click to expand the full script and action notes. The goal is to feel like a product demo, not a university presentation.
01 The hook — open on the problem 0:00 – 1:30 ▶
Action

Open with the pitch deck on slide 2 — "Discovery sees behaviour. It doesn't predict it." Do NOT open the dashboard yet.

Say this

"Discovery already collects more behavioural data than almost any company in Africa. Every step tracked, every meal scanned, every kilometre driven. But today, most of that data tells you what already happened. You track behaviour. You score it. You reward it. And then... you wait for something to go wrong."

Continue

"Behavioural decline is predictable. The signals are there 7 to 14 days before the outcome. The gym visits start dropping. The sleep shifts. The app opens less often. The food choices change. Nobody intervenes — because nobody's watching for the pattern. That's the gap we're closing."

Tip

Pause for one full second after "that's the gap we're closing." Let it land. Don't rush to the next line.

02 Introduce LifeOS — the platform overview 1:30 – 3:00 ▶
Action

Switch to the browser. Open the Dashboard page at localhost:5173. Let it render fully before speaking.

Say this

"This is Discovery LifeOS — a unified behavioural intelligence platform. Not an app. Not a chatbot. An intelligence layer that sits on top of Vitality, HealthyFood, and Discovery Insure — and makes everything predictive instead of reactive."

Point at the KPI strip

"Right now it's monitoring five users. It's fired interventions proactively. And you can see, even at the platform level, the risk arc: baseline — decline — recovery. That arc only looks clean because something intervened during the decline."

Action

Scroll down slowly to the counterfactual chart. Point at the amber gap region.

On the chart

"The amber region is the value of early intervention — the risk points prevented. Averaged across all users, that gap is 13.9 risk points per day in the recovery period. That's directly translatable to actuarial outcomes."

03 The user story — navigate to USR_001 3:00 – 5:30 ▶
Action

Click through to Users → USR_001 (High Performer). Let the page load. Point at their name and risk badge.

Say this

"Let me show you a real story. This user — let's call them a high-performer, Diamond Vitality, active, engaged — starts declining in week 7. Slowly. Invisibly. Sleep drops 42 minutes a night. Gym visits halve. App engagement falls. Nobody notices."

Action

Click the "Risk" tab on the chart — point at the composite risk line rising from day 42 onward.

On the chart

"Our model picks up this trajectory before the user consciously notices it. That dashed red line at day 71 — that's when the early warning fires. 7 days before the risk peaks. AUC of 0.988 — near-perfect advance detection."

Action

Switch the chart to "Wellness" view. Point at the sleep line dropping.

Continue

"Watch the signals. Sleep goes first. Then activity. Then nutrition. Then driving behaviour. Cross-domain fusion catches what no single-domain model can — because this isn't a health problem or a nutrition problem. It's the same underlying stress event showing up across all three."

04 Claude explains — the live AI moment 5:30 – 7:00 ▶
Action

Scroll to the three Claude panels. Click "Ask Claude to explain →". Watch text stream in.

While it streams — say this

"The model doesn't just produce a number. Every risk score comes with an explanation — generated live by Claude. Watch the text arrive. This is what the user would see in the Discovery app. Not a score. A story."

After it loads

Read the first sentence of the explanation aloud. Then say:

Say this

"That's not a template. That's the model reading this specific person's behavioural trajectory and writing a personalised explanation. SHAP values tell Claude which signals mattered most. Claude turns that into something a human can actually act on."

Tip

If the streaming is slow, that's fine — use it. Say "you can see the AI thinking in real time." The streaming effect is actually a feature, not a delay.

05 Interventions — the load-shedding moment 7:00 – 8:30 ▶
Action

Click "Generate interventions →". While loading, toggle the Load-shedding switch ON in the top right.

Say this

"The intervention selector doesn't just pick a recommendation. It's a contextual bandit — it learns which lever works for this person, at this moment. Watch what happens when I toggle load-shedding on."

Action

Click "Generate interventions →" again with load-shedding on. Point at how recommendations change.

On the change

"The gym recommendation disappears. Home workout. Ready-to-eat HealthyFood options. The system knows Eskom's schedule and adapts. No global platform has this. This is South African intelligence."

Tip

Point at the reward shown on each card — "500 Vitality points", "25% HealthyFood discount". These are real Discovery products. This is not a concept — it's an integration spec.

06 The counterfactual — the killer moment 8:30 – 10:30 ▶
Action

Click "Show counterfactual →". Pause. Let the chart animate in fully before speaking.

Say this — slowly

"This is the question every Discovery executive should be asking. Not 'what happened?' — but 'what would have happened?'"

Point at the two lines

"The teal line is what happened — with intervention. The dashed red line is the counterfactual — where this user was heading without it. The gap between them is what LifeOS creates. That gap has a number: 71% probability of full Vitality disengagement in 6 weeks. Without action."

The close

"With the recommended interventions, this user's trajectory stabilises within 2–3 weeks. 58% reduction in insurance claim risk. That's not a wellness metric. That's an actuarial outcome. That's the language Discovery speaks."

Do NOT say

"This is just a prototype / simulation / demo data." Say "synthetic data" if asked — but don't volunteer it. The model logic is real. The architecture is real. The intervention framework is real.

07 The close — the ask 10:30 – 12:00 ▶
Action

Switch to pitch deck slide 10 — "The Ask". Stand still. Don't click anything else.

Say this

"Discovery already has the data. Discovery already has the distribution. LifeOS is the prediction and intervention layer that makes it all proactive instead of reactive."

The three asks

"We're asking for three things: 3 months of engineering partnership to integrate with live Vitality and HealthyFood pipelines. 1 pilot — a cohort of Discovery employees, real data, real outcomes. And 1 measurable result: a reduction in Vitality disengagement and claims risk within 90 days."

The final line — memorise this

"The data already exists. The infrastructure already exists. The users already exist. All that's missing is the intelligence layer that makes it predictive. That's what we built."

Timing tip

If you're over 12 minutes, cut Beat 5 (load-shedding) to a single line mention instead of a live demo. Everything else is non-negotiable.

Judge Q&A prep
Tough questions — answered
Click each question to reveal the answer. Practise saying these out loud before the competition.
Q"Doesn't Discovery already do this with Vitality?"
Vitality tracks and rewards behaviour that already happened. LifeOS predicts behaviour that hasn't happened yet and intervenes before the negative outcome occurs. Vitality is a lagging indicator system. LifeOS is a leading indicator system. They're complementary — we make Vitality smarter, not redundant.
Q"How do you handle POPIA and cross-product data sharing?"
Consent by design. Users explicitly opt in to cross-product signal fusion with granular controls. Each data source requires separate consent. Users can opt down — reducing data sharing granularity while still receiving degraded predictions. Every data access is logged, timestamped, and auditable. The architecture treats privacy as a first-class constraint, not an afterthought.
Q"This is synthetic data — how do we know it works on real data?"
The model architecture, feature engineering, and intervention logic are production-grade — they'd work identically on real Vitality and HealthyFood data. The synthetic data was generated to match real behavioural distributions documented in wellness research. The AUC of 0.988 would naturally be validated on a real cohort in the pilot phase. We're proposing exactly that as the next step.
Q"Why XGBoost? Why not a deep learning model?"
Three reasons: Interpretability — XGBoost with SHAP values gives us per-prediction explanations that regulators and users can understand. Data efficiency — gradient boosted trees outperform deep learning on tabular data with hundreds, not millions, of users. Production readiness — XGBoost is battle-tested at scale. We'd graduate to a Temporal Fusion Transformer as data volume grows, but XGBoost is the right choice for this problem size.
Q"What's the commercial model? How does Discovery make money from this?"
Three revenue mechanisms: Reduced claims — every prevented Vitality disengagement reduces long-term health claims exposure. Premium retention — users who stay engaged don't lapse. HealthyFood GMV — contextual food discounts increase HealthyFood transaction volume. The counterfactual shows 58% claim risk reduction — even a fraction of that at scale is a significant actuarial saving.
Q"Could Discovery build this internally? Why do they need you?"
Discovery absolutely could — and we're not claiming otherwise. What we're offering is speed and focus. We've built the prediction and intervention layer in a hackathon timeframe. The question isn't capability — it's prioritisation. Large organisations move slowly on greenfield innovation. We've de-risked the concept, validated the architecture, and built a working demo. The 3-month pilot we're proposing answers the commercial question with real data.
Q"What makes this different from other health AI companies?"
Three differentiators no other platform has: Cross-product signal fusion — Vitality + HealthyFood + Insure in one model. No standalone health app has telematics and grocery scan data. The Discovery integration — we're not building a consumer product, we're building an intelligence layer on top of existing infrastructure that has 4 million+ users. South African context — load-shedding integration, HealthyFood retailer network, local route intelligence. This is locally defensible against global competitors.
Loom recording guide
Recording a backup demo video
Always record a Loom before the live demo. If anything fails — wifi, API key, a crash — you have a flawless video to fall back on. Judges respect the backup.
Before recording
  • Restart both backend and frontend fresh
  • Clear all Claude panels (hard refresh the page)
  • Set browser zoom to 100%
  • Close all other browser tabs
  • Test audio — say "Discovery LifeOS demo" and play it back
  • Set Loom to record screen + camera if possible
  • Turn off all notifications
  • Target 8–10 minutes for the Loom (tighter than live)
What to record (in order)
  • Open on Dashboard — 30 sec overview of platform stats
  • Counterfactual chart — point at the amber gap (20 sec)
  • Navigate to USR_001 — introduce the user story (60 sec)
  • Switch chart views — wellness → risk (30 sec)
  • Click "Ask Claude to explain" — let it stream (45 sec)
  • Toggle load-shedding on → generate interventions (45 sec)
  • Click "Show counterfactual" — read the numbers aloud (60 sec)
  • End on the counterfactual chart — let it sit for 5 seconds
  • Final line: "That gap is the value of early intervention."
Recording tips
  • Speak slower than you think you need to — 20% slower
  • Pause 2 seconds after clicking anything — let it load on screen
  • Don't say "um" — pause silently instead
  • Narrate what you're clicking before you click it
  • Point your cursor at what you're talking about
  • If you make a mistake — stop, deep breath, continue. Don't restart.
  • Record at least 2 takes. Use the second one — you'll be warmer.
After recording
  • Watch the full recording before uploading
  • Check audio is clear throughout
  • Trim the start and end (Loom has basic editing)
  • Set the title: "Discovery LifeOS — GradHack Demo"
  • Copy the shareable link — have it ready on your phone
  • Also download the MP4 as an absolute backup
  • Share the link with all team members before the day
When things go wrong
Recovery plays
Something will go wrong. It always does. This section tells you exactly what to do so you don't freeze.
🔴 Backend won't start
Switch to Loom video immediately. Say: "Let me show you the recorded demo while we sort the connection." Play the Loom. Continue your pitch script verbally over the video. Judges understand technical issues — they care about your composure.
🔴 Claude API times out or returns an error
The fallback responses are built into claude_service.py — they activate automatically when the API fails. The UI looks identical. If judges ask: "We have a fallback layer — the architecture is the same, the API just returns a cached response under load."
🟡 The chart doesn't load / shows blank
Hard refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R). If still blank, switch to the pitch deck slide 7 — it has the same counterfactual chart as a native PowerPoint chart. Narrate from there. Say "you can see the same pattern here in the deck."
🟡 You forget a number mid-pitch
Say "let me pull that up" and point at the model performance card on the dashboard — it shows all four metrics. Never make up a number. Judges remember made-up numbers.
🟡 You're running over time
Cut Beat 5 (load-shedding toggle) to a single mention: "The system adapts to load-shedding schedules — gym becomes home workout, cooking becomes ready-to-eat." Then jump straight to the counterfactual. Never rush the close — it's the most important beat.
🟢 A judge interrupts with a question mid-demo
Answer it in one sentence, then say "I'll show you that in a moment" and continue the demo script. If they press further, answer fully — but use the Q&A section language. Never let a question derail the story arc.
